Chapter 4: Election of Administrators

Article 23: Administrators of the community shall be elected by vote, with a maximum of three administrators.

Article 24: If no one stands for election, no further elections will be held until the next election period.

Article 25: Elected administrators must belong to a specific group, which is regarded as the government.

Article 26: Elected administrators are not allowed to make decisions alone. Decisions must be made in consultation with the government.

Article 27: It is prohibited to leak government information to the outside.

Article 28: The founders of the Community shall not directly grant the authority of the Community to the elected administrator. The administrator and the founder must consult with each other. The implementation of the results of the consultations must also be carried out by the founders.

Article 29: Founders of communities are prohibited from exercising dictatorship.

Article 30: After a one-month term, government members, excluding founders, must resign.

Article 31: Citizens have the right to raise objections to this text if they disagree.
