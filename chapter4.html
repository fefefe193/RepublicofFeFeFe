Article 23:

Administrators of the community shall be elected by vote, with a maximum of three administrators.

Article 24:

Those who have participated in the community for one week may stand for the position of administrator.

Article 25:

If no candidates stand for election, no further elections shall be held until the next election period.

Article 26:

Elected administrators must belong to a specific group, which is recognized as the government.

Article 27:

Elected administrators are not permitted to make decisions independently. All decisions must be made in consultation with the government.

Article 28:

The leakage of government information to external parties is prohibited.

Article 29:

The founders of the community shall not directly delegate the authority of the community to the elected administrator. The administrator and the founder must consult with each other, and the implementation of the results of these consultations must also be carried out by the founders.

Article 30:

Founders of the community are prohibited from exercising dictatorship.

Article 31:

After a one-month term, government members, excluding founders, must resign.

Article 32:

Government members, like all citizens, are entitled to the same rights and are subject to the same obligations as citizens. Thus, government members are equally subject to penalties as citizens and must endure the same punishments.

Article 33:

Regarding administrators who violate the rules, the community founders shall have the authority to impose warnings and penalties without requiring the permission of the national government.

Article 34:

Citizens have the right to raise objections to this text if they disagree.
